NCBI repositories and FAIR criteria

Open Research Data by Charité scientists in 2020

For 2020, 260 research articles written by Charité scientists were identified that shared the underlying research data freely in a data repository. Of these 300 articles, 120 used one or more NCBI repositories to share their data. Gene Expression Omnibus in particular is a frequently used repository by Charité scientists. 88 articles have shared their research data in this repository.

NCBI repositories and completeness of FAIR criteria

The analysis of published research data included a qualitative assessment of FAIR criteria completeness using automated assessment tools like F-UJI. The analysis demonstrated that the NCBI repositories do not meet the FAIR criteria. They achieve an overall score of 4.2% just by passing the test “Identifier is resolvable and follows a defined unique identifier syntax (IRI, URL)”. All other FAIR tests failed.

While field-specific repositories generally show a low completeness of FAIR criteria, there are examples that score better. (For example the Electron Microscopy Data Bank or CodeOcean.)

The last chart shows the FAIR scores broken down by the four main FAIR principles (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable) and grouped by NCBI repositories, other field-specific repositories, and general-purpose repositories. It demonstrates that NCBI repositories only partly fulfill the Findable principle and completely fail on the other principles, whereas the general-purpose repositories on average also achieve high to medium scores on these principles.
